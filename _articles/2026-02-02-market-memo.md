### 7. Glossary of Financial Jargon
* **Adam & Eve Double Top:** A bearish reversal pattern consisting of a sharp, spike-like first peak ("Adam") followed by a more rounded, gradual second peak ("Eve"), suggesting buyers are losing exhaustion.
* **Gap Fill:** When price returns to a level where a "gap" (empty space on chart due to overnight move) occurred. Filling the gap and *reversing* is often a continuation signal for the original trend.
* **Topping Tail (Wick):** A candlestick with a long upper shadow, indicating that buyers pushed price up, but sellers aggressively pushed it back down by closeâ€”a strong bearish signal.
* **Class B Divergence:** A specific type of bearish divergence where price makes a double top (or slightly lower high) while the oscillator (RSI/MACD) makes a lower high, confirming momentum loss.
