### 7. Glossary of Financial Jargon
* **Adam and Eve Double Top:** A bearish reversal pattern featuring a sharp, V-shaped first peak (Adam) followed by a wider, rounded second peak (Eve), indicating a slow exhaustion of buyers.
* **Bearish Divergence:** Occurs when an asset's price records a higher high, but a momentum indicator (like MACD or RSI) records a lower high, signaling that the uptrend is losing strength and a reversal is imminent.
* **Broadening Formation:** A chart pattern identified by two diverging trendlines (higher highs and lower lows). It represents extreme volatility and lack of control from either bulls or bears, often seen at major market tops.
* **Fibonacci Retracement (78.6%):** A technical analysis tool used to identify potential support or resistance levels based on the Fibonacci sequence. The 78.6% level is often viewed as a deep pullback level before a trend invalidation.
* **Ichimoku Cloud:** A technical indicator that defines support and resistance, identifies trend direction, gauges momentum, and provides trading signals. A "red cloud into the future" indicates a projected bearish trend.
* **Margin Call:** A demand from a broker for an investor to deposit additional capital to cover potential losses. If unmet, the broker will forcefully liquidate the investor's assets, often causing cascading market selloffs.
* **Shooting Star:** A bearish candlestick pattern with a long upper shadow, little or no lower shadow, and a small real body near the low of the week. It appears after an uptrend and signals potential topping.
* **Stochastic Oscillator:** A momentum indicator comparing a particular closing price of a security to a range of its prices over a certain period. A drop below the 50 centerline on a weekly chart is a significant bearish momentum shift.
